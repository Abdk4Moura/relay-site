How it works · two modes

One controls booted machines. One controls everything else.

Relay is honest about the trade. Pick the mode that fits the target.

Free

App mode

Install the small open-source receiver on a booted machine. Your phone controls it over your own network, sees its screen, and moves files both ways.

  • Keyboard, mouse, and live screen over LAN
  • Two-way file transfer
  • Receiver is open source and auditable
DIY hardware or our dongle

Out-of-band mode

Tap the target's video with a capture card and emulate a USB keyboard from a tiny bridge. A Raspberry Pi today, our AI-KVM dongle later. Nothing runs on the target, so it works at the BIOS, the login screen, a TV, or a crashed OS.

  • Reaches the screen before any OS loads
  • Emulated USB keyboard, real HID
  • Open bridge firmware to build on
Open by default

You should be able to read the thing that types on your machine.

The phone app, the desktop receiver, the wire protocol, and the bridge firmware are all open source under GPLv3. The hosted relay, the fleet console, and the agent cloud are the paid services that keep the lights on.
