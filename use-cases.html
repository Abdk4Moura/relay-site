Use cases

One device. The reach of a keyboard, a remote, and a crash cart.

Practical at home, serious in the rack, and a new kind of tool for agents. Here is what people actually do with Relay.

01 · Living room

Type on your TV.

Searching a streaming app one letter at a time with a D-pad is the worst input method ever shipped. Point Relay at the TV and type in your search, your password, your account email, with a real keyboard.

Works over Bluetooth with nothing installed on the TV, or out-of-band on sets that lock you out.

02 · Rescue

Fix a PC that won't even boot.

The machine is stuck at the BIOS, or sitting at a login screen, or the OS is dead. Remote desktop is useless here because there is nothing for it to run inside. Relay's out-of-band mode taps the video and emulates a keyboard, so you can drive the firmware itself from your phone.

A Raspberry Pi with a capture card today. Our AI-KVM dongle later.

03 · Media PC

Couch control your media PC.

The box under the TV does not need a wireless keyboard balanced on the armrest. Relay gives you a trackpad, a full keyboard, and the live screen on your phone, so you can scrub, search, and launch from across the room.

04 · On stage

Present without a clicker.

Your slides advance from your phone, your speaker notes sit in your palm, and a tap moves you forward. No dongle to lose, no batteries to forget, no walking back to the laptop.

05 · Agents

A body for your AI agent.

Computer-use agents can reason about a screen, but they need a way to reach machines they were never installed inside. Relay is that body. Through MCP, an agent sees the live screen and acts with real keyboard and mouse, even at the BIOS or on a box with no OS at all.

This is the mode that makes Relay more than a convenience. It is infrastructure for the agent era.

06 · Team

Run a fleet from one screen.

Racks of machines, kiosks across sites, lab boxes that never run the same OS twice. Relay gives your team one device directory with roles and an audit log, so any machine is one tap away and every action is accounted for.

Available on the Team plan.
